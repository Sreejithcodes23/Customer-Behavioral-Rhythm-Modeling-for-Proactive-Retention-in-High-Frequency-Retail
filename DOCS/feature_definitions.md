# Feature Definitions

This document describes the features stored in the `customer_behavior_score` table used for behavioral monitoring and retention analytics.

Each row represents a **customer snapshot for a specific date**.

---

## Table: customer_behavior_score

| Column | Description |
|------|-------------|
| userid | Unique identifier for the customer |
| snapshot_date | Date on which behavioral metrics are computed |

---

## Behavioral Features

### days_since_last_purchase

Number of days since the customer's most recent order.
days_since_last_purchase = snapshot_date − last_purchase_date

This measures the current progress in the customer's purchase cycle.

---

### mean_gap

Average number of days between purchases for the customer.

Computed using historical order intervals.

This represents the customer's typical purchase rhythm.

---

### predicted_npt

Predicted next purchase timing.

Represents the expected total gap between two consecutive purchases.

Predicted using a machine learning model trained on historical purchase behavior.

Example:

If `predicted_npt = 10`, the customer is expected to purchase approximately every 10 days.

---

### bds (Behavioral Deviation Score)

Measures how much the current purchase gap deviates from the expected rhythm.
bds = days_since_last_purchase / predicted_npt

Interpretation:

| BDS Value | Interpretation |
|----|----|
| < 1 | Purchase earlier than expected |
| ≈ 1 | On expected purchase rhythm |
| > 1 | Purchase delay |
| > 1.8 | Strong delay |

---

## Risk Segmentation

### risk_bucket

Behavioral risk classification derived from BDS.

| Condition | Risk Bucket |
|----|----|
| BDS < 0.8 | early |
| 0.8 ≤ BDS ≤ 1.2 | on_time |
| 1.2 < BDS ≤ 1.8 | mild_delay |
| BDS > 1.8 | high_delay |

This classification identifies customers who are deviating from their expected purchase behavior.

---

## Priority Features

### priority_score

Intervention urgency score computed as:
priority_score = bds × (1 / predicted_npt)

This prioritizes customers who:

- have high behavioral deviation
- are expected to purchase soon

Higher scores indicate higher retention priority.

---

## Customer Activity Metrics

### total_orders

Total number of orders placed by the customer historically.

---

### orders_last_30

Number of orders placed in the last 30 days.

Used to measure recent engagement.

---

### total_spend

Total historical revenue generated by the customer.

---

### avg_order_value

Average value of orders placed by the customer.
avg_order_value = total_spend / total_orders

---

### spend_last_30

Total spend by the customer during the last 30 days.

This captures recent customer value and engagement.

---

## Overdue Condition

A customer is considered overdue when:
days_since_last_purchase > predicted_npt

This means the customer has exceeded their expected purchase interval.

Overdue customers are strong candidates for proactive retention intervention.
